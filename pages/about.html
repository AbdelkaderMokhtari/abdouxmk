// ACT 01 — PRESENT
Who I am today
I'm a Builder & Founder based in Saida, Algeria. I design systems that solve real problems — not just ship features. My work sits at the intersection of backend architecture, mobile engineering, and product thinking.

My core stack is Laravel for backend APIs, Flutter for mobile, and Clean Architecture as a discipline across everything I build. When I take on a project, I'm not thinking about the next sprint — I'm thinking about what this system looks like at 10x scale.
👤 images/abdelkader.jpg
// ACT 02 — JOURNEY
How I got here
Self-taught in a market where the path isn't obvious. I started as most developers do — building things to learn. But somewhere along the way, I stopped asking "how do I build this feature" and started asking "why does this system need to exist, and how should it be designed to last?"

Algeria gave me a particular kind of problem set — real infrastructure gaps, real operational challenges, real markets that global SaaS products ignore. That became my advantage. I learned to design for constraints that force creativity, not comfort that breeds mediocrity.

Now in my final year of a Computer Science degree, I'm not waiting to graduate before building — I've been shipping production systems the whole time.
// ACT 03 — FUTURE
Where I'm going
The next chapter is about building products that matter in the Arab world — not replicating what already exists in the West, but designing systems for the specific infrastructure gaps, languages, and operational realities of this region.

AI-native productivity tools for Arabic-speaking knowledge workers is the space I'm moving into — tools that aren't just translated, but re-architected for how Arab professionals think and work. MindPilot is the first step. It won't be the last.
